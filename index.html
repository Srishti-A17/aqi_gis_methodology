Layer 1

Continuous Surface Analysis (IDW)

"Capturing spatial gradients and hotspots"

1

IDW Interpolation

Inverse Distance Weighted (IDW) interpolation applied to AQI point data to estimate continuous spatial variation across the entire study area. This method assumes that nearby locations have more influence than distant ones.

2

Raster Processing

  • Clip interpolated raster surface to New York State boundary
  • Convert continuous raster to point features for downstream spatial analysis

Analysis Purpose

Estimate continuous spatial variation of maximum AQI values across New York State, revealing pollution gradients and identifying geographic hotspots where air quality concerns are most severe.

Key Insight

IDW interpolation transforms discrete monitoring station data into a continuous surface, allowing us to visualize and analyze air quality patterns in areas without direct measurements.

Output

Continuous AQI Surface

A spatially continuous representation of maximum AQI values across NYS, enabling identification of pollution gradients and geographic hotspots.

Layer 2

Hexagonal Aggregation

"Standardizing spatial units & reducing edge bias"

1

Hexagonal Tessellation

Creation of uniform hexagonal grid covering New York State. Hexagons provide superior spatial aggregation compared to squares because each cell has equidistant neighbors.

2

Spatial Join

Join AQI point data to hexagonal features based on spatial location, associating each measurement with its containing hexagon.

3

Aggregation

Calculate summary statistics (mean, maximum, count) of AQI values within each hexagon for standardized comparison.

Analysis Purpose

Reduce point-level noise and variability by aggregating data into uniform geographic units, enabling standardized spatial comparison and pattern recognition across the state.

Why Hexagons?

Hexagonal grids reduce sampling bias from edge effects and provide more uniform spatial relationships between neighboring cells compared to square grids.

Output

Aggregated Hexagon Values

AQI statistics aggregated into uniform hexagonal units, facilitating fair spatial comparison and pattern analysis across regions.

Layer 3

Buffer-Based Proximity Analysis

"Assessing localized influence zones"

1

Buffer Zone Generation

Create concentric distance-based buffer zones around each AQI monitoring station at multiple radii (e.g., 5km, 10km, 15km) to examine spatial influence.

2

Distance-Decay Analysis

Compare AQI values and variability within each buffer ring to understand how air quality changes with distance from monitoring stations.

Analysis Purpose

Examine the spatial influence and proximity-based exposure patterns around monitoring locations, understanding how pollution impacts vary with distance from measurement sites.

Distance Matters

Buffer analysis reveals whether pollution effects are highly localized around sources or diffuse across broader regions, informing exposure assessment strategies.

Output

Proximity Exposure Patterns

Comparative analysis of AQI behavior within increasing distance bands, revealing spatial decay patterns and localized influence zones.

Layer 4

Voronoi (Thiessen) Polygon Analysis

"Population-normalized exposure analysis"

1

Voronoi Polygon Generation

Create Voronoi (Thiessen) polygons from AQI monitoring stations, clipped to NY State boundary. Each polygon contains all locations closer to its monitoring station than to any other station.

2

Population Data Integration

Spatially join census population data to each Voronoi zone, associating demographic information with air quality measurements.

3

Normalization Calculation

Calculate AQI exposure per million population for each zone, standardizing pollution burden by population density to identify disproportionate impacts.

Analysis Purpose

Normalize pollution exposure by population to identify areas with disproportionate environmental health burden, revealing environmental justice patterns and vulnerable communities.

Environmental Justice Lens

Population normalization reveals which communities face the highest per-capita pollution exposure, essential for equitable policy interventions and resource allocation.

Final Output

AQI Per Million Population

Population-normalized pollution exposure metrics that identify communities experiencing disproportionate environmental health risks relative to their size.
